# Relic Crafting

Skill requirements and gold costs for crafting every relic. Values up to Denial of the Believer come from Cyril's site; higher-skill entries are based on player experience and reports. Last updated 23 March 2021.

## How Skill Levels Work

- **Minimum skill** is the level at which you can first attempt a relic. Attempts at or near the minimum will often fail, and a failed attempt loses the gold spent.
- **Trivial skill** is the level at which the relic never fails — but crafting a trivial relic also never raises your crafting skill.
- To gain skill you must risk failure: craft relics that are above trivial-guaranteed range for you.

## Escalating Costs for 5k and 10k Relics

The relics priced at 5,000 or 10,000 gold have a special cost structure based on how many **pure** copies of that relic you own (relics attached to weapons, armor, or spells do not count):

- With zero pure copies, the cost is the listed 5,000 or 10,000 gold.
- Each additional pure copy you own multiplies the next attempt's cost by 10. Example: first Strong Arm costs 5,000; the second attempt costs 50,000; the third 500,000; escalating up to 500 million gold for the 6th copy of a 5k relic, and up to 1 billion gold for the 6th copy of a 10k relic.
- The relics do exactly the same thing regardless of cost — the escalation is purely to make crafting harder.
- To avoid the escalation, sell your pure relics back to the store before crafting more of the same one.
- The 500 million and 1 billion gold relics do not escalate; their price is fixed.
- Other item types (armor, weapons, spells, bows, arrows) never increase in cost as you craft duplicates.

## Note on Treasure Elements and Leather of Delegation

- Treasure elements (EEoT and EEoGT) require **Reliccrafting, Metalworking, and Runeworking** — unlike other relics, which use Reliccrafting, Gemworking, and Runeworking.
- To make treasure elements you must first craft weapons or armor to train Metalworking.
- **Leather of Delegation** is an oddball: it uses **Leatherworking and Metalworking**, so you must first make armor, bows, or weapons to train those skills.
- Players who craft only relics will hit a wall on LoD and treasures unless they craft other item types first.
