# Beasts

## Overview

Beasts are special critters not available through the normal Actions, New Fight menu. They either appear randomly when awakened by a player action (almost any action can trigger it — fighting, chatting, crafting, walking, even playing poker) or are summoned with ash and gold at the **Ash Collector** (location 141,Hell,174). When a beast appears, a yellow system message announces its location to all players. Anyone who reaches the beast before it dies can fight it via the Actions, Battle menu. Beasts are much harder to kill than normal creatures and can drop unique, valuable items.

- Type **/beast** in game to list the beasts your character has killed so far in the current month.
- The Ash Collector stands on magma in Hell: summoning requires Hell access AND a Templar's Wand (permanent Templar). 10-day trial access alone is not enough; permanent Templar plus trial access works.
- The Ash Collector is not to be confused with the **Ash Keeper** at 150,Sur,150.
- As of 6/14/2008 the summoner also needs **Aqualungs**.
- Since June 2008 all private (summoned) beasts appear on the Surface; public summoned beasts still appear on the various planes.
- Private beasts require far more ash than public ones, and only the summoner sees a private beast's location.

## Damage Resistance

Beasts resist damage far more than normal creatures, and more than keep, puddle, and pub critters. For fixed or dur-based relic damage (relics like VV or BP):

- Pub, puddle, keeps: about 25x less damage than in normal kingdoms.
- Regular approbation beasts (Widow, Corpse, EHA): about 8x less than pub damage.
- Higher beasts like Rogues: about 10x less than the regular beasts.
- Example: 1 billion damage to a normal Yoro = 40 million to Pub Yoros, 5 million to an EHA, 500k to a Goblin Rogue.
- Polymorphic Beetles are easier than approbation beasts — roughly 3x less damage than the Pub (about 13 million in the example above).
- Per Fire Chicken (S2): for Bloodletters (vamps with 67%+ of base points in Dur and Fangs of the Bloodletter), relic damage to beast guards equals damage to regular beasts, and **uber beasts take 400x less relic damage than regulars**.
- DotB appears to do less damage as a beast dies even while its remaining health is well above yours, which seems at odds with its description (lowers enemy health by 90% or by 90% of your durability, whichever is lower).

## Approbations and Blessings

Some beasts grant an approbation or blessing — a relic permanently attached to a body part. They take no storage space, are always equipped, and can greatly increase your damage.

- A character can hold **10 approbations and blessings total** (10 combined, not 10 of each).
- They do not show anywhere on your character; type **/app** in game to see yours.
- The Polymorphic Beetle was the first beast added, is the easiest non-surface beast, and gives a **blessing**.
- **Approbations** come from: Crucified Corpse, Darkest Widow, Eclyptic Hell Angel, plain Lord Alchemar, and Rogue Goblin.
- **No blessing or approbation** from: Palace Alchemars, Ancient Alchemar, Master Templar, Grandmaster Templar, Goblin Shaman, Golem of Living Rock, Unborn Torments, Dragon Eaters, and the 3 easy Sur beasts (Goblin Miner, Goblin King, Goblin Thief).

## Reaching and Fighting Beasts

- Since the **Beast Bane** (BB) and **Beast Noobane** (BNB) were added, public beasts are nearly impossible to get without one: holders teleport instantly to 1 kingdom from the beast and usually kill it within seconds. Manual teleporting rarely gets there in time, though it can happen if you are quick and lucky — odds improve late in the month.
- Only 4 beasts ever appear as public spawns on Sur; all other public spawns are on the four other planes (unless summoned privately). Three surface beasts (Goblin King, Thief, Miner) are very easy, meant to give newbies and non-access players a beast kill — but you effectively need at least a Noobane to reach them in time.
- Older beasts have high defensive skill (lowers your damage) and heal unless annulled. Best answer is a **Scepter of Annulment (SoA)**, though enough annulment relics also work.
- If you keep attacking any beast without enough annuls and it keeps healing, you are eventually **auto-muted** — and while muted you cannot attack any beasts. Every heal also broadcasts a message to the kingdom, so repeat healers make enemies fast and may get chainkilled.
- Older beasts have almost no evasion (Agility or Contravention), so a modified accuracy stat (Dex, Agi, or Cnc) of only around 100k can hit them.
- The hardest beasts: per the 5/17/04 announcement, the Lord Alchemar and all beasts more powerful than him carry their own Scepters of Annulment, making annul-able relics useless against them. Some also have high evasion — figure 3 bil modified Dex, Cnc, or Agi to hit them, 5 bil to hit every attack, and bring your own SoA. Exception: the Ancient Alchemar, Lord Alchemar, and Palace LA reportedly have low evasion and can be attacked with low accuracy (per Dragonmage, S2).

### Difficulty Tiers

The source color-codes beasts; tiers used in the table below:

- **Older** — the original beasts; easier than some newer ones, but need annuls/SoA to stop healing.
- **Surface** — the only beasts that appear randomly as public beasts on the Surface (all privately summoned beasts also land on Sur). Miner, Thief, and King are easy; the Rogue is hard and needs an SoA.
- **Hardest** — black-text beasts: own SoA, often high evasion.

### Kill Messages

The kill message identifies which beast died (format roughly "Joe Schmo has slain the **** Beast"). For old beasts, the keyword tells them apart: a plain "Beast" is a Beetle, "Ultra Beast" is an EHA, and so on. Newer beasts have explicit kill messages. The 3 types of UT share one kill message, as do the 3 types of PLA and 3 types of Dragon Eater — to tell an Ice from a Storm etc. you must target the beast with animated graphics enabled: flames, lightning, or snow are superimposed on its image.

## Kill Restrictions

- You can kill only **one of each beast per month**. After killing a DW you cannot even attack another DW until the beast lists reset at the Acumen; other beasts are still fair game.
- Killing an **Ancient Alchemar** blocks you from all "regular" beasts (Beetle, Corpse, Widow, EHA, UT, other Alchemar flavors) that month — and killing any regular beast blocks the AA.
- The three 7,500-ash beasts (DW, CC, EHA) are summoned randomly as privates: you cannot pick which appears, so you may summon a duplicate you cannot attack.
- Think before summoning: if you already killed that beast this month, you cannot attack the one you summon. The **/beasts** command (added early 2018) lists your month's kills to avoid this.
- Newer "irregular" beasts appear separate from the AA-vs-regular rule: Dragon Eaters, Golems, and Shamans are believed attackable even after an AA kill, but the author is not certain — do not summon on that assumption. How Master Templars, Grandmaster Templars, and Rogues interact with AA restrictions is not fully known (see per-beast notes).

### Polymorphic Beetle (Beetle, PB)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Beast at...
- Easiest non-surface beast to kill. Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: may drop an Ocean Bubble (confirmed by Metalg316 on S2, but not a sure thing per Swp Devil on S2), Shirt Ripper (per Niizandar), and Apex (per Sarenrae on S2).

### Crucified Corpse (Corpse, CC)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Super Beast at...
- Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: sometimes a tecton.

### Darkest Widow (Widow, DW)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Mega Beast at...
- Requires a **RDADMS Lessonbook** to attack. Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: sometimes a tecton.

### Eclyptic Hell Angel (EHA)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Ultra Beast at...
- Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: the Tradeskill Manifesto.

### Unborn Torment (UT)

- Comes in 3 flavors: Fire, Lightning, Ice (same kill message for all).
- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Horrid Beast at...
- Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: an Ice, Fire, or Lightning element every time; may also drop Demonic items (verified).

### Lord Alchemar (LA)

- Kill message: Killed the Lord Alchemar at...
- Requires a **Scroll of Forbidden Rights** to fight. Needs more accuracy than the older beasts, though evasion is reportedly low. Its head becomes Quickening.
- Possible to kill without an SoA (Mav73 did it with 15 annuls as relics and approbations) but easier with one. It carries its own SoA, so your annullable relics will not work.
- Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month — but you CAN fight one after killing a Palace Lord Alchemar.
- Drops: can drop Demonic items (confirmed by Emiren on S2), Sacrificial Skillation and Devoid relics (per Agromon on S1).

### Palace Lord Alchemar (PLA)

- Spawn message: A Winged beast is spotted flying from the Palace! Kill message: [Playername] has protected the Forbidden Palace with valor! (messages confirmed 9 Aug 2021)
- Requires a **Scroll of Forbidden Rights** to fight. Does not require high accuracy.
- Testing by Mav73 (S1): 13 annuls equipped were needed to kill; with fewer, its heals recovered all damage. The heals gave no in-game warnings (no mute risk) but kept it from dying.
- Cannot be attacked if you already killed an AA that month.
- Drops: Palace Elements (Storm, Blood, or Lava) and "amazing wealth" (4 EEoGT).

### Ancient Alchemar (Ancient, AA)

- Spawn message: A winged beast flees the mines With runes! Kill message: [Playername] has gained treasure for slaying the Ancient at... (messages confirmed 10 Aug 2021)
- Requires a **Scroll of Forbidden Rights** AND a **Beastbane** to attack. Low evasion reported; carries its own SoA, so annullable relics will not work.
- If you killed a regular beast (Beetle, CC, DW, EHA, UT, LA, or PLA) you cannot fight AAs that month. Since September 2006, only one AA kill per month.
- Drops: can drop EEoGT, EEoT, Demonic items, Earthen items, Devoids; drops between 2 and 8 palace elements (random count).

### Rogue Goblin (Rogue)

- Spawn message: An Evil Presence Lurks About At... Kill message: Killed the Rogue Goblin at...
- Sur beast, but hard to kill — an SoA (or 13 Annulment relics against its 25 relics) is needed. Low evasion like the older beasts, so accuracy needs are modest. Unlikely for non-access players.
- AA interaction: killing a Rogue does NOT block an AA (Cagedangel and Bravo Mischef, S2; Almez, S2 killed an AA after a Rogue) — but Almez could not attack a Rogue after killing an AA, so an AA kill appears to block Rogues. Do NOT summon a private Rogue after killing an AA that month.
- Drops: can drop Demonic or Earthen items; also a Goblin Head, convertible into 999 runes.

### Master Templar (MT)

- Spawn message: [Playername] has awoken a beast near [location]. Kill message: [Playername] has slain the Master Templar and receives a free skill increase [location] (messages confirmed 10 Aug 2021)
- Best killed with an SoA. Not excluded by killing an AA.
- Reward: a free increase to the skill selected for training (haste, criticality, etc.).
- Drops: Earthen (Manchester, S2) and Crystal (Tyraelthefallen, S1) items, most likely Demonic as well.

### Grandmaster Templar (GMT)

- Spawn message: Awakened A Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Grandmaster Templar at...
- Best killed with an SoA. Not excluded by killing an AA (per Nsane, S1).
- Reward: 1-point increases to various tradeskills (runeworking, weapon crafting, etc.), randomly chosen. Author's best was 12 skills; average 9 skills across 94 GMTs on both servers; as few as 5 reported (Bernie Sanders, S2).
- Drops: Crystal (author on S1, Carlcts on S2), Earthen (Chesty Puller, S2), and Demonic (Lord Damarus, S2) items.

### Goblin Shaman (Shaman)

- Spawn message: The Stench Of Rotting Flesh Fills The Air... (per Macca123). Kill message: Killed the Goblin Shaman at...
- Does not require an SoA (demonstrated by Archangel Michael on S2 with a level 1,000 character), but it does have relics to annul: The Forsaken Dark (S1) annulled 15 relics doing 9x9 damage, while Averlily without an SoA had the Shaman heal fully on every attack.
- Not excluded by killing an AA (per Archer Of Crosis, S1).
- Drops: Warrior's Squire, Caster's Familiar; can drop a hide for the Goblin Hide Backpack or its head (convertible into 999 runes); sometimes Earthen (James2773, S2) or Crystal (Swp Devil, S2), probably Demonic too.

### Golem Of Living Rock (Golem, GoLR)

- Spawn message: The ground shakes and the clamouring of chains can be heard in the distance. Kill message: [playername] has slain a Golem Of Living Rock at... (messages confirmed 10 Aug 2021)
- Best killed with an SoA. Not excluded by killing an AA (per Archer Of Crosis, S1).
- Drops: Archer's Aid, Theurgal Orb; can drop Crystal, may drop Demonic, Earthen, and Devoids (per Lord Ragnarok).

### Dragon Eater (DE)

- Comes in 3 flavors: Fire, Thunder, Ice (same kill message for all).
- Spawn message: A shrill screech sounds in the distance followed by a horrendous growl...and then all is silent (per Jmo, S2). Kill message: [Playername] has slain a Dragon Eater at...! (messages confirmed 10 Aug 2021)
- Best killed with an SoA, though Shiny Pete (S2) killed 3 different DEs with only 8 Annuls each — an option before you have an SoA.
- Not excluded by killing an AA (per Dr Zues, S1).
- Does NOT spawn from the 1,000 or 2,250 ash public summonings; the private summon consumes 30,000 ash.
- Drops: an Essence Element of Dragon Flames, Storm, or Shards depending on which type spawns; may also drop Crystal, Demonic, or Earthen items.

### Goblin Thief (Thief)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Goblin Thief at...
- Sur beast, 10 Dur, no annuls needed — anyone can kill it. Not excluded by killing an AA (per Nsane, S1).
- Cannot be summoned (only randomly awakened) and does not spawn from the 1,000 or 2,250 ash public summonings.
- Drops: treasure elements; can drop Demonic items or Devoids (verified by Macca123, S2).

### Goblin Miner (Miner)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Goblin Miner at...
- Sur beast, 10 Dur, no annuls needed — anyone can kill it. Not excluded by killing an AA (per Nsane, S1).
- Cannot be summoned (only randomly awakened) and does not spawn from the 1,000 or 2,250 ash public summonings.
- Drops: Ash; can drop Demonic items (verified).

### Goblin King (King)

- Spawn message: Awakened a Beast At... Kill message: Killed the Goblin King...
- Sur beast, 410 Dur, no annuls needed. Not excluded by killing an AA.
- Damage cap: **3 damage per attack per player** (only 1 for pure vamps unless they add offensive items — each equipped weapon, damage spell, and relic set does 1 point, so a weapon plus damage spell, 2 damage spells, or 2 weapons alongside relic damage reaches 3).
- Vamp tip: with 2 shields and a rapid damage spell, equip a second damage spell instead of a heal, then attack with the fireball icon or the C key for 3 damage points per attack.
- Getting the kill against other attackers is luck — you cannot tell when it is down to its last 1–3 hitpoints. Keep attacking and hope.
- Cannot be summoned (only randomly awakened) and does not spawn from the 1,000 or 2,250 ash public summonings.
- Drops: Free Fortifier; can also drop Devoid (per Agromon) or SS relics and Demonic items.

### Nightmare Fly (Fly)

- Spawn message: A waking nightmare fills your vision (partial — a location follows). Kill message: [Player] has killed the Nightmare Fly and took some body parts at xxx,Sur,yyy!
- Sur beast; listed Dur 2 billion(?) but it only takes small damage like the other slow-kill Sur beasts. The author doubts 2 bil is real, since a ~150 damage cap would mean over 13 million attacks.
- Probably no SoA needed (like the Zombie), probably no evasion, but likely to heal off a low-level attacker.
- Damage cap of roughly 150 per attack per player when relics and two attacks are combined. Author's observed maxima: 40 relic damage (46 with a surge) and 30 per cast (35 with a surge).
- Not available as private; only via the 1 bil + 1,000 ash and 1.5 bil + 2,250 ash public summons.
- Drops: Goldblum Goo, which starts a somewhat tedious quest; can drop Demonic items (Drakken Deathlord, S1, 12 Mar 2018) and probably Devoids; can drop Sacrificial Skillation.

### Dragonling Zombie (Zombie)

- Spawn message: An abomination fails to take flight. Kill message: [Player] has killed the Dragonling Zombie and took some body parts at xxx,Sur,yyy!
- Sur beast; listed Dur 2 billion(?) with the same caveat as the Fly (only takes small damage).
- No SoA needed (per Maz, S1). No apparent evasion — a low-level mule cast without fizzling — but the Zombie healed most times against weak attacks.
- Works like the Fly: damage cap around 150 per attack per player. Keep attacking and hope.
- Not available as private; only via the 1 bil + 1,000 ash and 1.5 bil + 2,250 ash public summons.
- Drops: Deformed Wing, which starts a somewhat tedious quest; likely Demonic items (if it follows the Fly's rules); can drop Devoids (per Badjuju, S2).

## Beast Summoning

The Ash Collector is at **141,Hell,174** on magma: you need Hell access, a Templar's Wand (permanent Templar), and Aqualungs. All private beasts appear on the Surface (since June 2008) and can land in surface water kingdoms — the intended killer should also have Aqualungs, or a water spawn will be unreachable.

Procedure: give the AC 1 gold to start the dialog. He eventually offers, for slightly less than 2 billion gold and a hunk of ash, to summon different types of beasts and asks you to offer an amount. The exact gold amount offered selects which private beast is summoned (it appears somewhere on Sur). Warning: if you carry more than about 7,000 ash and offer a listed amount, the AC summons the beast immediately — he does not merely "tell you what he can do".

| Beast to Summon | Gold to Give the AC | Ash Used |
|---|---|---|
| Goblin Shaman | 1,999,999,999 | 8,500 |
| Grandmaster Templar | 1,999,999,998 | 7,000 |
| Golem of Living Rock | 1,999,999,997 | 8,500 |
| Master Templar | 1,999,999,996 | 8,000 |
| Rogue Goblin | 1,999,999,995 | 7,200 |
| Polymorphic Beetle | 1,999,999,994 | 7,500 |
| Dragon Eater (consumes 30,000 ash) | 1,999,999,993 | 30,000 |
| Random Crucified Corpse, Darkest Widow, or Eclyptic Hell Angel — you cannot choose which, and a duplicate of one you already killed that month is unattackable | 2,000,000,000 | 7,500 |
| Any random public beast, success guaranteed | 1,500,000,000 | 2,250 |
| Any random public beast, success NOT guaranteed | 1,000,000,000 | 1,000 |

## Surface Beast Notes

- The Rogue Goblin, Goblin Miner, Goblin Thief, and Goblin King all appear on the surface and can be reached instantly with the **/bn** command (requires a Beast Noobane).
- The Miner and Thief have only 10 Dur and are easily killed by anyone who reaches them in time. The Rogue is much harder. The King has only 410 HP but a damage cap of 1 per attack per attack type (weapons or hands, spells, and relics can each do 1 point), so the kill is mostly luck.
- The Fly and Zombie have much higher Dur than the Goblins but similarly take very little damage per attack, and can heal even against an SoA. Killing them takes persistent attacking over a relatively long time, and against competing attackers the final kill is chance.
